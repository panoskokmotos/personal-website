About Me

Entrepreneur building for social impact

Born in Patras, Greece, I grew up in a low-income family — which taught me to value the small, important things in life and live with purpose over comfort. Watching my family come home tired and unmotivated was a crucial mindshift: I committed to getting educated, understanding how the world works, and taking risks.

I studied Mechanical Engineering at the University of Patras (Top 3%), spent a semester at UPC Barcelona on Erasmus, and turned that foundation into a mission: contribute to a better tomorrow.

I co-founded Givelink in 2022 — an in-kind donation platform where donors shop for exactly what nonprofits need. No cash abstraction, just real goods, real impact, real stories.

Beyond Givelink, I host Entrepreneurship Talks, serve as a WEF Global Shaper in San Francisco, and sit on the Executive Board of Social Impact SF. Ultimately, I want to provide value to every young person facing economic difficulties — and help them believe in their future.

Reading

Books That Shaped Me

Every year, I read almost 40 books. Physics, philosophy, biology, entrepreneurship — the ones I keep coming back to.

🗺️

I think that our mind is like a Google Map. We should make sure there are fewer areas that are pixelated — so from the youngest age possible we want to build an accurate map of the world. The best way to do that is to consume as much information as possible, which is easiest through books. Through this we create a mental model of the universe, and the more accurate that model is, the better our decision making becomes.

Every night, 1 hour before going to bed, I stop everything and just take a book to read. This makes me relax, reflect on my day, enriches my experience, and sets me up for the next.
